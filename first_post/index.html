About me

As I don’t want to dox myself, I will not be giving you guys my full name, but you can call me Afrokilla. I’m also known as afrokilla#8343 on Discord and @afrokilla on Twitter. Yes, I’m that guy who spams giveaways and comments, but I also post about up-and-coming NFT projects (sometimes). Anyway, I work as a programmer with a degree in computer science engineering and after my day job I spend most of my time on Discord servers grinding for those whitelists and minting/trading NFTs.

Why I started this blog

I had been thinking of starting a blog for a while now, initially it would be more related to programming and feature tutorials and stuff, but I recently amassed a huge Twitter following of 103 people with my crypto account and felt I cannot disappoint my loyal followers.

I feel there is much gatekeeping in the space, especially for people new to NFTs & crypto. For myself it was also difficult to get started, hard to find new projects, to get whitelisted, to know if a project was good or not. Luckily, by random chance, I met some great guys in the RetroWolves discord voice chat. One thing led to another and we made or own “Alpha Discord” to discuss projects, share alpha, and crack some jokes.

I have learned much more from our small community than I would have ever learned on my own in the same timeframe. It essentially allows everyone involved to benefit from each other’s research and experience. Therefore, I started this blog to help people find good projects, tools, and resources. I want to be that “friend” to aid you through your Web3 journey and perhaps build a small community of likeminded people on my Twitter.

So what content can we expect?

The focus of the blog will be on Decentralized Finance (DeFi), mainly focused on NFTs, Web3 and how it works, and a bit of programming & tutorials to interact with Web3.

  • Write-ups about new NFT projects
  • Tools and resources
  • News from the NFT space
  • Basics of crypto & NFTs
  • Web3 programming

A work in progress

As you may have noticed, the blog still has some bugs & glitches, but I’m working on it. I felt it was time to start writing instead of coding and setting everything up. I hope you will follow me through my Web3 journey, learn some new things, and perhaps make some new friends 😄
